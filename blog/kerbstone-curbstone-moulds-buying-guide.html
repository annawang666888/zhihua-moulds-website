Kerbstone & Curbstone Moulds Buying Guide

How to choose road curb, side stone, L-type, S-type, slope and radius kerbstone moulds for municipal roads, medians and landscaping projects.

Kerbstone moulds and curbstone moulds are widely used for municipal roads, sidewalks, traffic medians, parking lots, parks, residential communities and landscaping boundaries. For overseas buyers, choosing the right mould starts with the local road profile, not only the product name.

Zhihua supplies kerbstone, curbstone, road curb and side stone moulds for standard catalogue products and OEM profiles from drawings or sample photos.

1. Confirm the local kerb profile

Different markets use different words for similar products: kerbstone, curbstone, road curb, side stone, road edge stone or concrete kerb. Before asking for a quote, confirm the finished concrete profile and installation purpose.

  • Slope kerbstone moulds: used where a sloped surface is needed for road edges, parking areas or drainage transitions.
  • L-type curbstone moulds: common for road edges, sidewalks and boundary applications.
  • S-type side stone moulds: often used for medians, separators and local road standards.
  • Straight kerb moulds: suitable for long road sections and simple boundary lines.
  • Radius kerbstone moulds: needed for curves, corners, islands and turning sections.

2. Measure the finished concrete product

For road curb moulds, the critical dimensions are length, height, top width, bottom width, slope angle, radius and chamfer. If your project follows a government or road-authority standard, send the cross-section drawing first.

If only a sample is available, provide clear photos from the front, side and top. Adding a ruler or known-size reference helps the factory judge the dimensions more accurately.

3. Straight pieces and radius pieces should match

Many road projects need both straight kerbstones and curved kerbstones. The radius corner piece should match the straight profile, otherwise the line may look inconsistent after installation. When requesting a quote, tell the supplier the curve radius or turning angle.

4. Choose standard catalogue or OEM moulds

Standard kerbstone moulds can reduce cost and delivery time when the dimensions already fit your market. OEM moulds are better when your local authority has a fixed profile, special chamfer, drainage edge or project-specific radius.

For OEM work, a drawing is best. If there is no drawing, a clear sample photo plus finished dimensions can still support an initial feasibility review.

5. Match kerbstones with drainage and paver products

Kerbstone moulds are a strong cross-sell with drainage channel moulds and concrete paver block moulds. Municipal roads, sidewalks and landscaping projects often need road curbs, drainage covers and paving slabs together.

For distributors and construction-material factories, a mixed product line can make the catalogue more complete and increase the chance of repeat orders.

6. What to send for a fast quote

  • Finished kerbstone length, height, top width and bottom width
  • Profile drawing, road-authority standard or clear sample photos
  • Type: slope, L-type, S-type, straight, radius or corner piece
  • Required radius or turning angle for curved sections
  • Application: road edge, median, sidewalk, parking area or landscaping
  • Order quantity, destination country and whether mixed profiles are needed

7. Avoid vague quote requests

A request such as “send kerbstone mould price” is usually too broad. A useful request includes the profile type, dimensions, quantity and photos. This avoids quoting the wrong mould and helps the supplier check whether a standard item can be used.
