Concrete Paver Block Moulds: Interlocking, Grass Paver and Paving Slab Guide

How to choose paving slab, grass paver, decorative paving stone and interlocking block moulds for your local market.

Concrete paver block moulds are used by construction-material factories, landscaping suppliers and contractors to produce paving products for sidewalks, plazas, courtyards, driveways, gardens and public spaces.

Because local markets often prefer different shapes and surface patterns, buyers should compare both standard catalogue moulds and OEM options. This guide explains the main choices before you request a quote for paver block moulds and paving slab moulds.

1. Choose by application first

A garden stepping stone, a plaza paving slab, a driveway interlocking block and a grass paver do not need the same mould structure. Start by deciding where the finished concrete product will be installed.

  • Pedestrian sidewalks and plazas: decorative paving slabs, interlocking pavers and square slabs are common.
  • Parking and green spaces: grass paver moulds and permeable grid blocks are more suitable.
  • Gardens and landscapes: round decorative stones, flower-pattern slabs and walkway pavers are popular.
  • Driveways: choose stronger interlocking patterns and confirm concrete thickness.

2. Confirm size and thickness

For pavers, thickness is just as important as length and width. Pedestrian products are often thinner than driveway or parking products. If your local market already has a popular product, send the finished paver dimensions and a photo.

3. Decide whether the pattern is standard or OEM

Standard patterns can reduce cost and delivery time. OEM patterns are useful when you need a local design, customer logo, special texture or a shape already accepted by your market. Zhihua can review sample photos or drawings before quotation.

4. Consider coloured concrete compatibility

Many paver products are produced with pigment-coloured concrete. Plastic moulds made from suitable PP or ABS materials are commonly used for red, yellow, grey, black and decorative paving products. Proper cleaning helps protect the mould surface and keep colours consistent.

5. What to send for a fast quote

  • Finished paver length, width and thickness
  • Pattern photo, surface texture or CAD drawing
  • Application: sidewalk, plaza, parking, driveway, garden or landscaping
  • Required quantity and destination country
  • Whether multiple patterns should be mixed in one shipment

6. Build a balanced product mix

For first-time importers, a mixed-SKU order can be more practical than buying only one pattern. A balanced mix might include interlocking pavers, grass pavers, square slabs and decorative stones. This lets you test local demand without committing to only one style.
