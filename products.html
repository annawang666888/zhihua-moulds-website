Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about manhole cover moulds, load classes, and specifications.

What is the difference between D400 and F900 manhole covers?

D400 is rated for 40-tonne loads (municipal streets, standard highways) while F900 is rated for 90-tonne loads (airports, heavy industrial). F900 requires 20-30% more concrete per cast and 30-40% higher mould cost.

Can you make manhole moulds with our logo?

Yes! We can include your logo or utility marking on the mould surface at no extra charge. Please provide your logo in high-resolution format (vector AI/EPS preferred).

What is the lifespan of a plastic manhole mould?

With virgin PP/ABS materials and proper usage, our moulds consistently deliver 300+ casting cycles. With proper care and maintenance, many last well beyond that.

What sizes of manhole moulds do you offer?

We offer standard round diameters of Ø 600 / 700 / 800 / 900 mm and square configurations. Custom sizes are available based on your drawings or local standards.

Kerbstone Mould FAQ

Common questions about kerbstone (curbstone) moulds, profiles, and applications.

What is the difference between L-type and S-type kerbstones?

L-type (L-shape) is used for road edging with a gentle 1:1 slope, while S-type (S-shape) is steeper and used for traffic medians. The choice depends on your road design and traffic requirements.

Can you produce coloured kerbstones with these moulds?

Yes, our moulds are compatible with pigment dyeing. Add pigment during concrete mixing to achieve various coloured kerbstone finishes (red, yellow, grey, terracotta, etc.) for aesthetic diversity.

What concrete grade do you recommend for kerb production?

We recommend C30/40 concrete for kerbstone production. Ensure proper curing and demoulding for optimal strength and surface finish.

Do you offer custom kerbstone dimensions?

Absolutely. Beyond our standard 1000 × 300 × 120 mm, we accept custom drawings for any length, height, or profile to match your local road authority specifications.

Paver Block Mould FAQ

Common questions about paver moulds, patterns, and concrete compatibility.

Which paver pattern is best for driveways vs. plazas?

Interlocking Z-shape and I-shape pavers offer the best load distribution for driveways and parking. Holland rectangular and decorative flagstone patterns are ideal for plazas, walkways, and pedestrian areas.

What is the standard thickness for paver blocks?

Standard thicknesses are 60 mm (pedestrian areas), 80 mm (light vehicles), and 100 mm (heavy traffic). Our moulds can be made for any of these dimensions plus custom large-format slabs.

Are your paver moulds compatible with coloured concrete?

Yes. Our PP material does not react with iron-oxide pigments, so you can produce vibrant red, yellow, black, and earth-tone pavers without staining the mould.

How many cycles can a paver mould produce?

With virgin PP and standard vibration-table demoulding, our paver moulds reliably deliver 300+ cycles. Lifespan increases with proper cleaning between casts and avoidance of metal-edge demoulding tools.
